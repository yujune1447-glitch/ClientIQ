# CreatorIQ — Master Brief

*A single source of truth for what CreatorIQ is, why it exists, and where it's going. Paste this into any new chat to get an assistant fully briefed. Pairs with `CLAUDE.md` (technical/build rules) in the repo.*

*Last updated: July 2026*

---

## 1. What CreatorIQ Is — in one line

CreatorIQ unifies YouTube Studio, Meta Business Suite, and TikTok Studio into one place, with an AI layer that generates **specific, structured content briefs** — exact title, hook, length, and outline — grounded in your own channel data and what's trending across all three platforms. Not generic ideas. The actual blueprint for the next thing you make.

**The gap it fills:** No product today unifies all three platforms *and* layers real, channel-specific AI intelligence on top. Competitors do one platform, or many platforms with no AI, or AI that stops at topic-level suggestions.

---

## 2. The Vision

Systemize going viral across every platform, in one place. Today a serious creator juggles three separate dashboards, guesses what to make next, and has no memory of whether their guesses worked. CreatorIQ becomes the single operational brain that (a) sees all your platforms at once, (b) tells you exactly what to make next and why, and (c) learns from what you actually shipped — so its advice sharpens the longer you use it.

**Cross-platform is the essence of the product, not a feature.** The whole reason to exist is seeing and acting across YouTube, Instagram, and TikTok at once — so the product should go to market *as* a cross-platform tool, not a single-platform one.

The long game: become the place a creator *plans their week*, not just gets ideas — the operational hub from idea → brief → calendar → "here's what I'm making," so leaving means rebuilding their whole workflow.

---

## 3. The Problem It Solves

The core pain, in the founder's own words: *"I didn't know what to make that would trend — specifically what title, what hook, what structure, what length — based on what I was already producing and what's already working out there."*

Break it into three real problems:

1. **The blank-page problem.** Creators don't know what to make next. Existing tools give vague ideas ("make a video about X"), not the executable specifics (title, hook, length, outline) that actually determine whether a video performs.

2. **The fragmentation problem.** Growth now happens across YouTube, Instagram, and TikTok simultaneously, but every analytics and planning tool is siloed to one platform. Nobody sees the creator's full cross-platform picture, so nobody can spot what's working *across* platforms.

3. **The no-memory problem.** Creators (and the tools they use) never close the loop. You get an idea, maybe you make it, and nothing tracks whether it landed or feeds that back into better recommendations. Every suggestion starts from zero.

CreatorIQ attacks all three: structured specifics, unified across platforms, with a feedback loop that remembers.

---

## 4. The Solution

- **Handle the strategy so creators can focus on creating.** Analytics-driven content-structure generation — not merely ideas, but the exact specifics — tailored to your channel and where you want to take it.
- **Systemize going viral in one place,** with analytics-based tracking across all platforms, internal (your own data) and external (cross-platform trends).
- **Close the loop:** track which ideas actually got made and how they performed, and feed that back into smarter recommendations over time.

---

## 5. Who It Serves

**Primary (beachhead): serious individual creators who want to grow.** People treating content as a career or serious pursuit, already posting, hungry to optimize. The founder *is* this user — the product is built from that pain.

**Secondary (higher-value): businesses, agencies, and multi-account creators.** Brands running growth across platforms, agencies managing multiple creators/clients, teams that need multiple accounts and collaboration. This is where the real revenue is.

**Strategic note:** serve both consumer and B2B, but don't split focus prematurely. Win the individual-creator beachhead first, then expand up-market into B2B where willingness-to-pay is higher. Consumer = free/low tier for reach and data; B2B = paid tiers that fund the business.

**Go-to-market / distribution (updated):** The founder is deliberately **not** marketing this to his faith-based YouTube audience — it doesn't fit, and that audience isn't the ICP anyway. Instead, the beachhead is his **startup circle and content-creator friends** — a warmer, far more ICP-aligned early cohort (they *are* serious creators and operators), plus a **purpose-built marketing channel** (a new build-in-public presence dedicated to CreatorIQ). This is arguably a stronger wedge than the faith audience would have been: the people being sold to are the exact users the product is for.

## 7. Defensible Advantages & Moats

**Live now / near-term:**

1. **Structured briefs, not ideas.** The product outputs the exact title, hook, optimal length, and outline — grounded in your own top-performer patterns. The immediate differentiator.
2. **Six-layer channel intelligence.** Analysis across Packaging, Retention (including an external "vs all YouTube" retention benchmark), Growth, Audience, Cadence, and Trajectory — synthesized into a plain-English "what works on your channel" that grounds every brief. VidIQ shows stats; CreatorIQ shows *what to do because of them.*

**Compounding moats (the uncopyable ones):**

3. **The feedback loop.** Track whether a saved idea got made and how it performed, then feed that back into the AI. This creates a proprietary "which of *our* ideas actually worked" dataset — per creator, and eventually across creators. A competitor can't copy this overnight because they'd need your users' history, not just your code.
4. **The deepening creator profile.** An accumulating model of voice, audience, and what's been tried, so recommendations sharpen with use. A switcher arrives at VidIQ with none of this context — cold-start is the moat.
5. **Cross-platform trend correlation.** With ≥2 platforms live, patterns *across* YouTube/IG/TikTok become more valuable than any single platform's trend data. No competitor can claim this — and it's now positioned to land *at* launch, not after.
6. **Aggregate cross-creator intelligence (network effect).** With volume, patterns across many creators make the product better for everyone — the one advantage that literally improves as more users join.
7. **The operational hub.** Owning idea → brief → calendar → "what I'm making this week" raises switching cost from "swap an app" to "rebuild my workflow."

**Founder-specific:**

8. **An ICP-aligned network.** Rather than leaning on his (off-ICP) faith audience, the founder goes to market through his startup circle and content-creator friends — the exact users the product serves. A tighter, higher-signal early cohort than a general audience: real creators and operators who can give sharp feedback and convert.
9. **Build-in-public via a dedicated channel.** A new marketing presence built specifically around CreatorIQ's journey creates a community/trust moat competitors structurally can't match — and, unlike a borrowed audience, it's aligned to the product from day one.

## 8. Weaknesses & Risks to Cover (be honest about these)

- **Cross-platform-first launch is gated by approvals the founder doesn't control.** The decision to ship as a cross-platform product (not YouTube-only) is strategically sound — cross-platform is the essence — but it means launch now depends on getting a second platform live. TikTok production access has a ~2-week approval lag (plus a current privacy/terms URL-verification issue to resolve); Instagram is blocked on Meta developer verification with **no timeline**. **Mitigation:** define launch as "≥2 platforms live," realistically YouTube + TikTok. Do **not** let Instagram (the open-ended blocker) hold the whole launch hostage — add it when Meta clears.
- **Distribution is being built from scratch.** By (rightly) not using the faith audience, the founder gives up a ready-made 100k-person launch pad. The startup-circle + creator-friends + new-channel approach is more ICP-aligned but smaller and slower to start. Building the marketing channel early is now a real workstream, not an afterthought.
- **Data cold-start.** The strongest moats (feedback loop, cross-creator intelligence) need user volume and outcome history that don't exist yet. They compound later but deliver little on day one. **Mitigation:** ship early to start the data flywheel; don't build the AI-consumption side before there's data to consume.
- **Platform/API fragility.** YouTube has hard limits (per-video CTR unavailable, public subscriber counts rounded, captions expensive on quota, daily quota caps). Meta and TikTok gate access behind approvals. The product's breadth is only as reliable as three third parties allow.
- **AI trust is fragile.** If the briefs cite shaky patterns or over-claim on thin data, credibility collapses. **Mitigation:** statistical honesty is a core principle — median not mean, confidence-gate small samples, never present a coincidence as a formula.
- **Solo-founder bandwidth.** One person building, selling, marketing, and supporting. Focus and ruthless scope discipline are survival traits. Beware building instead of shipping.
- **Monetization unproven.** Will individual creators pay? VidIQ's pricing is the benchmark to test against. The free/paid line and the paid value prop still need sharpening.
- **Low switching cost until the hub exists.** Until CreatorIQ owns weekly planning, a user can leave with little friction. Getting to operational-hub status is what locks retention.

## 10. Current Build State (as of July 2026)

**YouTube — essentially a complete product:**
- Full OAuth, persistent login, data pull, analysis pipeline, live in production.
- **Six-layer Channel Analysis** built and verified: Packaging, Retention (incl. external relativeRetentionPerformance benchmark), Growth (subs + traffic), Audience (demographics + comments), Cadence, Trajectory — plus an AI cross-layer synthesis ("what works on your channel") that grounds Planning Content.
- **Planning Content:** chat-based flow that opens with data-grounded concept suggestions and generates full structured briefs (Title / Hook / Optimal Length / Outline / Why it'll work), each citing real channel patterns.
- **Saved Ideas:** Supabase-backed kanban (To Make / In Progress / Done) with card editing and delete.
- **Feedback loop Phase 1** (idea_outcomes capture): link a Done idea to a posted video and snapshot its performance from cached data. Phases 2–3 (re-grading, feeding outcomes back into AI) deferred until real outcome data accrues.
- **Quota-safe incremental fetch** (re-runs ~32 units vs ~5,000) and a **Recompute** path that regenerates the whole analysis from cached data with zero API calls.
- **Dashboard/Overview:** unified 5-row view; weekly growth uses exact Analytics-API subscribersGained (public counts are rounded).

**Instagram:** OAuth scaffolded, BLOCKED on Meta developer/SMS verification (support ticket pending, no timeline). The open-ended blocker.

**TikTok:** ACTIVE workstream and the key to launch. Sandbox app "ContentCreatorIQ" with Login Kit; OAuth code built (connects, returns test-account data — currently all zeros). Remaining: production API application (screen recording + submission; verifier currently can't validate the privacy/terms URLs — likely a vercel.app-subdomain or crawler-rendering issue to resolve), then real data once approved.

Deployed on Vercel. Tech stack: Next.js (App Router), Tailwind, Supabase, Claude API, YouTube Data + Analytics APIs, Instagram Graph API, TikTok for Developers API.

## 11. How It Keeps Improving (Roadmap)

**Launch strategy (updated): ship cross-platform, not YouTube-only.** Because cross-platform is the essence of the product, launch when ≥2 platforms are live — realistically **YouTube + TikTok**. Do not wait on Instagram (open-ended Meta block); add it as the third platform when it clears.

**Now → get to a second platform:**
- **TikTok is the critical path.** Resolve the privacy/terms URL verification issue, submit the production application (starts the ~2-week clock — do this first), build the TikTok data + token-refresh layer and UI shell so it lights up the moment approval lands.
- **Instagram:** keep the Meta ticket alive, but treat as passive/wildcard — never the launch gate.
- **YouTube:** already launch-quality; verify the two open items (watch-time card, weekly growth) on a fresh pull.
- **Marketing channel:** stand up the new build-in-public presence and start warming the startup-circle + creator-friends cohort *before* launch.

**Launch (≥2 platforms live):** ship the cross-platform product to the startup circle + creator friends. Cross-platform trend correlation — the VidIQ-differentiating wedge — is now part of the launch story, not a later add-on.

**Post-launch, as data accrues:** Instagram when Meta clears (third platform); feedback loop Phase 2 (verdict re-grading) and Phase 3 (feed outcomes into Planning Content grounding); the deepening creator profile; then aggregate cross-creator intelligence.

**Ongoing:** sharpen brief quality, light UI/aesthetic pass, and move toward the operational-hub vision (planning + calendar) to raise switching cost.

## 12. Strategic Principles

- **Ship cross-platform, launch on the first two that are ready.** Cross-platform is the essence, so don't launch single-platform — but don't let the open-ended blocker (Instagram/Meta) hold the whole launch hostage either. YouTube + TikTok is enough to prove the thesis; Instagram follows.
- **TikTok is the critical path to launch.** Everything bends toward getting a confirmed second platform live. Submit the production app first — the approval clock is the one thing you can't speed up later.
- **Market to the ICP, not a convenient audience.** Go to market through the startup circle, creator friends, and a purpose-built build-in-public channel — the people the product is actually for — not the faith audience.
- **Users before features.** The biggest risk is building instead of shipping. Polish enough to be credible, then ship and improve live. Start the data flywheel early.
- **Statistical honesty over impressive output.** Median not mean; confidence-gate small samples; never present a coincidence as a formula. Trust is the product.
- **Don't chase parity where you can't win.** Live stats is parity at best — keep it lean. Invest in the wedge (structured briefs, feedback loop, cross-platform intelligence).
- **Sequence the moats to when they're real.** Cross-platform correlation needs platforms; feedback/cross-creator intelligence needs data. Build capture early, turn on consumption when there's substance.
